An estimate of 1 million Syrians are physically injured, and roughly 80,000 need prosthetic fittings to replace limbs lost to the war. In the Undergraduate Interaction Design Department at California College of the Arts, we are exploring the growing refugee crisis and how products and services might be designed and leveraged for the continued migration of millions of people. We are looking at creating an on-the-ground training service to help those who need prosthetic fittings. This is a team project created by Nathaniel Smith, Andres Hurtado, Jennifer Kim , and Weiwei Hsu in Undergraduate Interaction Design 4: Experience.
